# German A1.1 — Articles (Artikel)

> German nouns always have a grammatical **gender**: masculine (der), feminine (die), or neuter (das). Articles change form depending on gender, number, and **case** (Nominative, Accusative, Dative).

---

## 1. Grammatical Gender (Genus)

Every German noun has one of three genders. You must memorize the article with each noun.

| Gender | Article | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Masculine (maskulin) | **der** | der Mann (the man) |
| Feminine (feminin) | **die** | die Frau (the woman) |
| Neuter (neutral) | **das** | das Kind (the child) |
| Plural (Plural) | **die** | die Männer / die Frauen / die Kinder |

### Tips for Guessing Gender

| Rule | Gender | Examples |
|------|--------|---------|
| Male people & professions | masculine | der Vater, der Arzt, der Lehrer |
| Female people & professions | feminine | die Mutter, die Ärztin, die Lehrerin |
| Endings `-er`, `-el`, `-en` (often) | masculine | der Lehrer, der Schlüssel, der Wagen |
| Endings `-ung`, `-heit`, `-keit`, `-schaft` | feminine | die Wohnung, die Freiheit, die Freundschaft |
| Endings `-chen`, `-lein` (diminutives) | neuter | das Mädchen, das Häuschen |
| Endings `-ment`, `-um` | neuter | das Instrument, das Zentrum |

> **Important:** Tips are helpful but have exceptions. Always learn the article with the noun!

## 2. Definite Article — Bestimmter Artikel ("The")

Used when referring to something **specific** or already known.

### Nominative (Wer? / Was?)

| Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **der** Mann | **die** Frau | **das** Kind | **die** Kinder |

- `Der Hund ist groß.` → The dog is big.
- `Die Katze schläft.` → The cat is sleeping.
- `Das Buch ist teuer.` → The book is expensive.

### Accusative (Wen? / Was?) — Direct Object

| Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **den** Mann | **die** Frau | **das** Kind | **die** Kinder |

> Only masculine changes: `der` → `den`

- `Ich sehe **den** Mann.` → I see the man.
- `Er liebt **die** Frau.` → He loves the woman.
- `Sie kauft **das** Buch.` → She buys the book.
- `Wir mögen **die** Kinder.` → We like the children.

### Dative (Wem?) — Indirect Object

| Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **dem** Mann | **der** Frau | **dem** Kind | **den** Kindern |

> Dative plural: add `-n` to the plural noun if it doesn't already end in `-n` or `-s`

- `Ich gebe **dem** Mann ein Buch.` → I give the man a book.
- `Ich helfe **der** Frau.` → I help the woman.
- `Das Spielzeug gehört **dem** Kind.` → The toy belongs to the child.
- `Ich danke **den** Kindern.` → I thank the children.

### Complete Definite Article Table

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **Nominative** | **der** | **die** | **das** | **die** |
| **Accusative** | **den** | **die** | **das** | **die** |
| **Dative** | **dem** | **der** | **dem** | **den** |

> **Memory trick:** In accusative, only masculine changes (`der` → `den`). In dative, everything changes.

## 3. Indefinite Article — Unbestimmter Artikel ("A / An")

Used when referring to something **non-specific** or mentioned for the first time.

### Nominative

| Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **ein** Mann | **eine** Frau | **ein** Kind | *(no article)* |

- `Das ist ein Hund.` → That is a dog.
- `Ich habe eine Schwester.` → I have a sister.
- `Er kauft ein Auto.` → He buys a car.

### Accusative

| Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **einen** Mann | **eine** Frau | **ein** Kind | *(no article)* |

- `Ich sehe **einen** Hund.` → I see a dog.
- `Sie kauft **eine** Tasche.` → She buys a bag.
- `Er liest **ein** Buch.` → He reads a book.

### Dative

| Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **einem** Mann | **einer** Frau | **einem** Kind | *(no article)* |

- `Ich helfe **einem** Freund.` → I help a friend.
- `Er gibt **einer** Frau Blumen.` → He gives a woman flowers.
- `Das Spielzeug gehört **einem** Kind.` → The toy belongs to a child.

### Complete Indefinite Article Table

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **Nominative** | **ein** | **eine** | **ein** | — |
| **Accusative** | **einen** | **eine** | **ein** | — |
| **Dative** | **einem** | **einer** | **einem** | — |

## 4. Negative Article — Negativartikel (`kein / keine`)

`kein` means **"no," "not a," or "not any"**. It negates a noun.  
`kein` follows the exact same pattern as `ein/eine`.

### When to use `kein` vs. `nicht`

| Use `kein` | Use `nicht` |
|-----------|------------|
| Before a noun with `ein/eine` | To negate a verb or predicate |
| Before a noun with no article | Before adjectives or adverbs |
| `Ich habe kein Geld.` (no money) | `Ich gehe nicht.` (don't go) |
| `Ich habe keinen Hunger.` (no hunger) | `Das ist nicht gut.` (not good) |

### Declension of `kein`

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **Nominative** | kein | keine | kein | keine |
| **Accusative** | keinen | keine | kein | keine |
| **Dative** | keinem | keiner | keinem | keinen |

### Examples

- `Ich habe **kein** Auto.` → I have no car. (neuter, nom.)
- `Das ist **kein** Problem.` → That is not a problem. (neuter, nom.)
- `Ich habe **keine** Zeit.` → I have no time. (feminine, nom.)
- `Ich sehe **keinen** Mann.` → I don't see a man. (masculine, acc.)
- `Es gibt **keine** Milch.` → There is no milk. (feminine, nom.)
- `Ich habe **kein** Geld.` → I have no money. (neuter, nom.)
- `Sie hat **keine** Kinder.` → She has no children. (plural, nom.)

## 5. Possessive Articles — Possessivartikel

Possessive articles show **ownership**. They follow the same declension pattern as `ein/eine`.

### Base Forms (Nominative, before declension)

| Pronoun | Possessive Article | Meaning |
|---------|------------------|---------|
| ich | **mein** | my |
| du | **dein** | your (informal) |
| er | **sein** | his |
| sie (she) | **ihr** | her |
| es | **sein** | its |
| wir | **unser** | our |
| ihr (you all) | **euer** | your (plural) |
| sie (they) | **ihr** | their |
| Sie (formal) | **Ihr** | your (formal) |

> **Note:** `ihr` can mean "her", "their", or "your (formal — Ihr with capital I)" — context determines the meaning.

### Possessive Article Declension (using `mein` as example)

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **Nominative** | mein | meine | mein | meine |
| **Accusative** | meinen | meine | mein | meine |
| **Dative** | meinem | meiner | meinem | meinen |

> All possessive articles follow this pattern: just replace `mein-` with `dein-`, `sein-`, `ihr-`, `unser-`, `euer-`.

**Note on `euer`:** When an ending is added, the `-e` in `euer` is often dropped:
- `euer` → `eu**r**e` (feminine nom./acc., plural)
- `euer` → `eu**r**em` (masculine/neuter dative)

### Examples with All Possessives

- `Das ist **mein** Haus.` → That is my house. (neuter, nom.)
- `Das ist **meine** Mutter.` → That is my mother. (feminine, nom.)
- `Ich liebe **meinen** Vater.` → I love my father. (masculine, acc.)
- `Ich helfe **meinem** Bruder.` → I help my brother. (masculine, dat.)
- `Das ist **dein** Freund.` → That is your friend.
- `Ist das **sein** Auto?` → Is that his car?
- `**Ihr** Hund ist sehr süß.` → Her dog is very cute. / Their dog is very cute.
- `Das ist **unser** Haus.` → That is our house.
- `Wo ist **euer** Lehrer?` → Where is your teacher? (plural you)
- `**Ihr** Name ist Müller.` → Your name is Müller. (formal)

## 6. Definite vs. Indefinite vs. Negative — Side-by-Side

| | Definite | Indefinite | Negative |
|--|---------|------------|---------|
| Meaning | the | a/an | no / not a |
| Masc. Nom. | **der** Hund | **ein** Hund | **kein** Hund |
| Fem. Nom. | **die** Katze | **eine** Katze | **keine** Katze |
| Neut. Nom. | **das** Kind | **ein** Kind | **kein** Kind |
| Plural Nom. | **die** Kinder | — | **keine** Kinder |
| Masc. Acc. | **den** Hund | **einen** Hund | **keinen** Hund |
| Masc. Dat. | **dem** Mann | **einem** Mann | **keinem** Mann |
| Fem. Dat. | **der** Frau | **einer** Frau | **keiner** Frau |

## 7. Contracted Articles (Verschmelzungen)

German frequently contracts prepositions with definite articles:

| Preposition + Article | Contraction | Example |
|-----------------------|-------------|---------|
| an + dem | **am** | `Ich bin am Bahnhof.` |
| an + das | **ans** | `Ich gehe ans Meer.` |
| in + dem | **im** | `Er ist im Kino.` |
| in + das | **ins** | `Wir gehen ins Restaurant.` |
| zu + dem | **zum** | `Ich gehe zum Arzt.` |
| zu + der | **zur** | `Er fährt zur Schule.` |
| von + dem | **vom** | `Ich komme vom Supermarkt.` |
| bei + dem | **beim** | `Er ist beim Arzt.` |

- `Ich gehe **ins** Kino.` → I am going to the cinema.
- `Sie ist **beim** Arzt.` → She is at the doctor's.
- `Wir fahren **zum** Bahnhof.` → We drive to the train station.
