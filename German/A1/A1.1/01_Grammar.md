# German A1.1 — Grammar (Grammatik)

---

## 1. Basic Sentence Structure (Satzbau)

German sentences follow a **Subject–Verb–Object (SVO)** pattern in their simplest form.

| Position | 1st | 2nd | 3rd+ |
|----------|-----|-----|------|
| Role | Subject | Verb | Object / Rest |

**Examples:**
- `Ich lerne Deutsch.` → I learn German.
- `Anna kauft ein Buch.` → Anna buys a book.
- `Wir essen heute Pizza.` → We eat pizza today.

---

## 2. The V2 Rule (Verb-Zweit-Regel)

> **The conjugated verb always occupies the second position in a main clause**, no matter what comes first.

This is the most important rule in German sentence structure.

```
[Position 1]  [Verb]  [Subject]  [Rest]
```

| Sentence | Position 1 | Verb (2nd) | Rest |
|----------|------------|------------|------|
| Ich lerne heute Deutsch. | Ich | lerne | heute Deutsch |
| Heute lerne ich Deutsch. | Heute | lerne | ich Deutsch |
| Deutsch lerne ich heute. | Deutsch | lerne | ich heute |

All three sentences mean the same thing. The verb `lerne` always stays second.

**More examples:**
- `Morgen gehe ich ins Kino.` → Tomorrow I go to the cinema.
- `Das Buch lese ich gern.` → The book I like to read.

---

## 3. Question Sentences (Fragesätze)

### Yes/No Questions (Ja/Nein-Fragen)
The verb moves to **first position**:

```
[Verb]  [Subject]  [Rest] ?
```

- `Lernst du Deutsch?` → Do you learn German?
- `Ist das dein Buch?` → Is that your book?
- `Hast du Zeit?` → Do you have time?

### W-Questions (W-Fragen)
A question word (W-Wort) takes position 1, verb stays 2nd:

```
[W-Word]  [Verb]  [Subject]  [Rest] ?
```

| W-Word | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Wer | Who | `Wer bist du?` (Who are you?) |
| Was | What | `Was machst du?` (What are you doing?) |
| Wo | Where | `Wo wohnst du?` (Where do you live?) |
| Woher | Where from | `Woher kommst du?` (Where are you from?) |
| Wohin | Where to | `Wohin gehst du?` (Where are you going?) |
| Wann | When | `Wann kommst du?` (When are you coming?) |
| Wie | How | `Wie heißt du?` (What is your name?) |
| Warum | Why | `Warum lernst du Deutsch?` (Why are you learning German?) |
| Wie viel | How much | `Wie viel kostet das?` (How much does that cost?) |

## 4. Negation (Verneinung)

### Using `nicht` (not)
`nicht` negates verbs, adjectives, adverbs, and specific nouns with a definite article.

- `Ich verstehe das nicht.` → I don't understand that.
- `Er ist nicht müde.` → He is not tired.
- `Wir gehen heute nicht ins Kino.` → We are not going to the cinema today.
- `Das ist nicht der Hund.` → That is not the dog.

**Position of `nicht`:**
- Before adjectives/adverbs: `Er ist **nicht** groß.`
- At the end of the sentence when negating the whole verb: `Ich schlafe **nicht**.`
- Before a specific element it negates: `Ich gehe **nicht** heute, sondern morgen.`

### Using `kein/keine` (no / not a)
`kein` negates nouns with indefinite articles or nouns without an article:

- `Ich habe keine Zeit.` → I have no time.
- `Das ist kein Problem.` → That is not a problem.
- `Ich habe keinen Hunger.` → I'm not hungry. (lit. I have no hunger.)
- `Er hat kein Auto.` → He has no car.

> **Rule:** Use `kein` where you would use `ein/eine`, and use `nicht` everywhere else.

## 5. TeKaMoLo — Adverb Word Order

**TeKaMoLo** is a mnemonic for the order of adverbial phrases in German:

| Letter | Stands for | Asks | Example |
|--------|-----------|------|---------|
| **Te** | **Te**mporal | When? (Wann?) | morgen, heute, um 8 Uhr |
| **Ka** | **Ka**usal | Why? (Warum?) | deshalb, wegen des Wetters |
| **Mo** | **Mo**dal | How? (Wie?) | mit dem Auto, schnell |
| **Lo** | **Lo**kal | Where? (Wo/Wohin?) | in Berlin, nach Hause |

**Order: Te → Ka → Mo → Lo**

**Examples:**
- `Ich fahre **morgen** (Te) **mit dem Bus** (Mo) **in die Stadt** (Lo).`
  → I travel tomorrow by bus into the city.
- `Ich gehe **heute** (Te) **wegen der Kälte** (Ka) **mit der Jacke** (Mo) **nach draußen** (Lo).`
  → Today, because of the cold, I go outside with my jacket.
- `Er arbeitet **täglich** (Te) **fleißig** (Mo) **im Büro** (Lo).`
  → He works diligently every day in the office.

> **Tip:** You rarely use all four elements at once. But when you use multiple, follow this order.

## 6. Subordinate Clauses (Nebensätze)

When you connect two ideas with a **subordinating conjunction**, the conjugated verb goes to the **end** of the subordinate clause.

**Common subordinating conjunctions (A1):**

| Conjunction | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| weil | because | `Ich lerne Deutsch, **weil** ich in Deutschland arbeiten **will**.` |
| dass | that | `Ich denke, **dass** du Recht **hast**.` |
| wenn | when/if | `**Wenn** ich Zeit **habe**, gehe ich spazieren.` |
| obwohl | although | `Ich lerne, **obwohl** ich müde **bin**.` |

**Structure:**
```
[Main clause], [conjunction] [subject] [rest] [VERB-at-end].
```

- `Ich bin müde, weil ich viel gearbeitet **habe**.` → I am tired because I worked a lot.
- `Ich weiß, dass sie Lehrerin **ist**.` → I know that she is a teacher.

---

## 7. Sentence Types Summary

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Statement | Subject + Verb + ... | `Ich trinke Kaffee.` |
| Yes/No Question | Verb + Subject + ... | `Trinkst du Kaffee?` |
| W-Question | W-word + Verb + Subject + ... | `Was trinkst du?` |
| Command (Imperativ) | Verb stem (+ ...) | `Trink Kaffee!` / `Trinken Sie Kaffee!` |
| Subordinate | ... weil/dass/wenn + ... + Verb | `..., weil ich Kaffee trinke.` |

---

## 8. Imperative (Imperativ) — Commands

| Form | Formation | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| du (informal) | Verb stem (drop `-en`) | `Lern Deutsch!` (Learn German!) |
| ihr (plural informal) | Verb stem + `-t` | `Lernt Deutsch!` |
| Sie (formal) | Infinitive + `Sie` | `Lernen Sie Deutsch!` |

- `Komm her!` → Come here! (du)
- `Hör zu!` → Listen! (du)
- `Schreiben Sie bitte Ihren Namen.` → Please write your name. (Sie)

---

## 9. Quick Reference: Sentence Checklist

1. Does the conjugated verb sit in **position 2**? ✓
2. In yes/no questions, is the verb **first**? ✓
3. After subordinating conjunctions, is the verb **last**? ✓
4. Are adverbials in **Te-Ka-Mo-Lo** order? ✓
5. Used `kein` for nouns and `nicht` for everything else? ✓
