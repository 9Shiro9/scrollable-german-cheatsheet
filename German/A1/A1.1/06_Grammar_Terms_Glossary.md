## Cases (Kasus / FĂ¤lle)

German uses four grammatical **cases** to show the role a noun plays in a sentence. The case is shown through the article or pronoun form.

---

### Nominativ (Nominative Case)

**Role:** The **subject** of the sentence â€” the person or thing performing the action.

**Question to ask:** `Wer?` (Who?) or `Was?` (What?)

**Articles:**
| Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| der / ein / kein | die / eine / keine | das / ein / kein | die / â€” / keine |

**Examples:**
- `**Der Mann** arbeitet.` â†’ The man works. (Who works? â†’ Der Mann)
- `**Eine Frau** lacht.` â†’ A woman laughs.
- `**Das Kind** schlĂ¤ft.` â†’ The child sleeps.

---

### Akkusativ (Accusative Case)

**Role:** The **direct object** â€” the person or thing directly receiving the action.

**Question to ask:** `Wen?` (Whom?) or `Was?` (What?)

**What changes:** Only **masculine** articles change: `der` â†’ `den`, `ein` â†’ `einen`, `kein` â†’ `keinen`

**Articles:**
| Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| den / einen / keinen | die / eine / keine | das / ein / kein | die / â€” / keine |

**Examples:**
- `Ich sehe **den Mann**.` â†’ I see the man. (See whom? â†’ den Mann)
- `Er kauft **einen Apfel**.` â†’ He buys an apple.
- `Sie hat **kein Geld**.` â†’ She has no money.
- `Wir brauchen **einen Arzt**.` â†’ We need a doctor.

**Common accusative verbs (frequently trigger accusative):**
`sehen` (see), `haben` (have), `kaufen` (buy), `lieben` (love), `essen` (eat), `trinken` (drink), `nehmen` (take), `brauchen` (need), `finden` (find), `kennen` (know)

---

### Dativ (Dative Case)

**Role:** The **indirect object** â€” the person or thing that receives something or benefits from the action.

**Question to ask:** `Wem?` (To/for whom?)

**Articles:**
| Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| dem / einem / keinem | der / einer / keiner | dem / einem / keinem | den / â€” / keinen |

> Dative plural: nouns add `-n` (if not already ending in `-n` or `-s`)

**Examples:**
- `Ich gebe **dem Mann** ein Buch.` â†’ I give the man a book. (Give to whom? â†’ dem Mann)
- `Er hilft **einer Frau**.` â†’ He helps a woman.
- `Das Spielzeug gehĂ¶rt **dem Kind**.` â†’ The toy belongs to the child.
- `Ich danke **meiner Mutter**.` â†’ I thank my mother.

**Common dative verbs (always or often take dative):**
`helfen` (help), `danken` (thank), `gehĂ¶ren` (belong to), `gefallen` (please/like), `geben` (give), `zeigen` (show), `schreiben` (write to)

**Dative prepositions (always use dative):**
`mit`, `nach`, `bei`, `von`, `zu`, `aus`, `seit`, `gegenĂĽber`

---

### Genitiv (Genitive Case) â€” Introduced at A1

**Role:** Shows **possession** or relationship between two nouns.

**Question to ask:** `Wessen?` (Whose?)

> At A1, possession is often expressed with `von + Dative` instead of the full genitive.

**Examples:**
- `Das ist das Auto **des Mannes**.` â†’ That is the man's car. (Genitive)
- `Das ist das Auto **von dem Mann** (= **vom Mann**).` â†’ That is the man's car. (common spoken substitute)
- `Der Name **der Frau** ist Anna.` â†’ The woman's name is Anna.

## Parts of Speech (Wortarten)

---

### Nomen / Substantiv (Noun)

A word that names a **person, place, thing, or idea**.

**Key features in German:**
- Always written with a **capital letter**: `der Hund`, `die Stadt`, `das GlĂĽck`
- Has a grammatical **gender**: masculine (`der`), feminine (`die`), neuter (`das`)
- Has a **plural form**: `der Hund` â†’ `die Hunde`

**Examples:**
- `**Das Haus** ist groĂź.` â†’ The house is big.
- `**Die Musik** macht mich glĂĽcklich.` â†’ Music makes me happy.

---

### Verb (Verb)

A word that expresses an **action or state**.

**Key features:**
- Must be **conjugated** to match the subject
- Sits in **second position** in main clauses (V2 rule)
- Moves to the **end** in subordinate clauses

| Type | Example | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| Action | `gehen`, `laufen`, `essen` | go, run, eat |
| State | `sein`, `haben`, `wissen` | be, have, know |
| Modal | `kĂ¶nnen`, `mĂĽssen`, `wollen` | can, must, want |
| Separable | `aufstehen`, `einkaufen` | get up, go shopping |

**Examples:**
- `Ich **lerne** Deutsch.` â†’ I am learning German.
- `Er **ist** mĂĽde.` â†’ He is tired.

---

### Adjektiv (Adjective)

A word that **describes a noun**.

**Key features:**
- Can come before a noun (attributive) or after a verb (predicative)
- When before a noun, adjectives take endings based on gender/case

**Predicative (after `sein`, `werden`, `bleiben` â€” no ending change):**
- `Der Hund ist **groĂź**.` â†’ The dog is big.
- `Das Essen ist **lecker**.` â†’ The food is delicious.

**Attributive (before a noun â€” A1 intro, full declension is A2):**
- `ein **groĂźer** Hund` â†’ a big dog
- `die **schĂ¶ne** Frau` â†’ the beautiful woman
- `ein **gutes** Buch` â†’ a good book

---

### Adverb (Adverb)

A word that **modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb**.

**Does not change form (no declension).**

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| Time (temporal) | heute, morgen, gestern, jetzt, immer, oft, manchmal, nie |
| Place (lokal) | hier, dort, ĂĽberall, zu Hause |
| Manner (modal) | schnell, langsam, gut, gern |
| Degree | sehr, zu, genug, fast |

**Examples:**
- `Ich lerne **gern** Deutsch.` â†’ I like learning German.
- `Er kommt **heute** nicht.` â†’ He is not coming today.
- `Sie spricht **sehr** schnell.` â†’ She speaks very quickly.

---

### Artikel (Article)

A small word placed before a noun that indicates the noun's **gender, case, and definiteness**.

| Type | German | English | Example |
|------|--------|---------|---------|
| Definite | der/die/das | the | `der Hund` |
| Indefinite | ein/eine/ein | a/an | `ein Hund` |
| Negative | kein/keine/kein | no / not a | `kein Hund` |
| Possessive | mein/dein/sein... | my/your/his... | `mein Hund` |

---

### Pronomen (Pronoun)

A word that **replaces a noun** to avoid repetition.

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Personal | ich, du, er, sie, es |
| Possessive | mein, dein, sein |
| Demonstrative | dieser, diese, dieses |
| Interrogative | wer, was, welcher |
| Indefinite | man, jemand, niemand |
| Reflexive | mich, dich, sich |

---

### PrĂ¤position (Preposition)

A word that shows the **relationship** between a noun and other elements.

**German prepositions always take a specific case:**

| Case | Common Prepositions |
|------|-------------------|
| Accusative | durch, fĂĽr, gegen, ohne, um |
| Dative | aus, bei, mit, nach, seit, von, zu, gegenĂĽber |
| Acc. or Dat. | in, auf, an, ĂĽber, unter, vor, hinter, neben, zwischen |

> Accusative = movement/direction (wohin?); Dative = location (wo?)

**Examples:**
- `Ich gehe **in die** KĂĽche.` â†’ I go into the kitchen. (accusative, movement)
- `Ich bin **in der** KĂĽche.` â†’ I am in the kitchen. (dative, location)

---

### Konjunktion (Conjunction)

A word that **connects** words, phrases, or clauses.

#### Coordinating Conjunctions (verb stays in position 2)

| German | English | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| und | and | `Ich lerne und du schlĂ¤fst.` |
| oder | or | `Tee oder Kaffee?` |

| German | English | Example |

| dass | that | `Ich denke, **dass** du nett **bist**.` |

| obwohl | although | `Ich komme, **obwohl** ich mĂĽde **bin**.` |

## Key Structural Terms

---

### Subjekt (Subject)

The **doer** of the action. Always in **Nominative** case.

- `**Ich** esse Pizza.` â†’ **I** eat pizza.
- `**Der Hund** bellt.` â†’ **The dog** barks.

---

### Objekt (Object)

The thing or person affected by the action.

- **Direktes Objekt (Akkusativ):** `Ich esse **eine Pizza**.` â†’ I eat **a pizza**.
- **Indirektes Objekt (Dativ):** `Ich gebe **ihm** die Pizza.` â†’ I give **him** the pizza.

---

### PrĂ¤dikat (Predicate)

The **verb** (or verb group) in the sentence.

- `Ich **lerne** Deutsch.` â†’ The predicate is `lerne`.
- `Er **hat gegessen**.` â†’ The predicate is `hat gegessen`.

---

### Infinitiv (Infinitive)

The **base form** of a verb, as found in a dictionary. Ends in `-en` (or `-n`).

- `lernen` (to learn), `gehen` (to go), `sein` (to be), `haben` (to have)
- Used after modal verbs: `Ich kann **schlafen**.`
- Used in Perfekt formation: `Ich habe ge**lernt**.`

---

### Partizip II (Past Participle â€” Verb 3)

The form of the verb used in the **Perfekt** tense.

| Regular | Irregular |
|---------|----------|
| ge- + stem + -t | ge- + stem (modified) + -en |
| gelernt, gekauft | gegangen, gefahren |

- `Ich habe Deutsch **gelernt**.` â†’ I learned German.
- `Wir sind nach Hause **gegangen**.` â†’ We went home.

---

### PrĂ¤teritum (Simple Past / Imperfect â€” Verb 2)

Used for written past tense. At A1, mainly `war` (was) and `hatte` (had).

- `Ich **war** gestern krank.` â†’ I was sick yesterday.
- `Er **hatte** keine Zeit.` â†’ He had no time.

---

### Perfekt (Present Perfect)

Used for spoken past. Formed with `haben` or `sein` + Partizip II.

- `Ich **habe** Kaffee **getrunken**.` â†’ I drank coffee.
- `Sie **ist** nach Hause **gegangen**.` â†’ She went home.

### Imperativ (Imperative)

The **command form** of a verb.

| Form | Example | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| du | `Komm!` / `Lern!` | Come! / Learn! |
| ihr | `Kommt!` / `Lernt!` | Come! / Learn! |
| Sie | `Kommen Sie!` / `Lernen Sie!` | Please come! / Please learn! |

### TeKaMoLo (Adverb Order)

| | Stands for | Question | Examples |

| **Lo** | Lokal | Where? (Wo?) | in Berlin, zu Hause, hier |

**Example:**
- `Ich fahre **morgen** (Te) **mit dem Zug** (Mo) **nach Hamburg** (Lo).`
  â†’ I travel tomorrow by train to Hamburg.

### V2-Regel (Verb-Second Rule)

The conjugated verb **always** takes the second position in a main clause.

```
[Position 1 â€” anything]  [VERB â€” always 2nd]  [Subject if not in pos.1]  [Rest]
```

- `Ich lerne heute Deutsch.` â†’ Verb `lerne` is 2nd.
- `Heute lerne ich Deutsch.` â†’ Verb `lerne` is still 2nd.
- `Deutsch lerne ich heute.` â†’ Verb `lerne` is still 2nd.

### Genus (Grammatical Gender)

Every German noun has a gender, shown by its article.

| Gender | Article | Hint |
|--------|---------|------|
| Maskulin | der | Male people, most professions |
| Feminin | die | Female people, `-ung`, `-heit`, `-keit` endings |
| Neutral | das | Diminutives `-chen`/`-lein`, many neuter endings |

### Kasus (Case)

| Case | German | Role | Question |
|------|--------|------|----------|
| Nominative | Nominativ | Subject | Wer? Was? |
| Accusative | Akkusativ | Direct object | Wen? Was? |
| Dative | Dativ | Indirect object | Wem? |
| Genitive | Genitiv | Possession | Wessen? |
